
ANTIMICROBIAL PEPTIDE EFFICACY STUDY - DATA DICTIONARY
========================================================

STUDY OVERVIEW:
Evaluation of AMP-247, a novel antimicrobial peptide, against Pseudomonas 
aeruginosa strains. Testing planktonic growth inhibition and biofilm 
prevention activity.

Goal: Determine minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) and anti-biofilm activity.

EXPERIMENTAL IDENTIFIERS
-------------------------
observation_id          Unique observation identifier
strain                  Bacterial strain (PAO1, PA14, Clinical_01-03)
concentration_ugml      AMP-247 concentration in µg/mL (0 = vehicle control)
treatment               Treatment category ("AMP-247" or "Vehicle")
timepoint_hrs           Hours post-inoculation (4, 8, 12, 24)
biological_replicate    Biological replicate number (1-3)
technical_replicate     Technical replicate number (1-3)
plate                   Assay plate identifier (batch effect source)

PRIMARY OUTCOMES
----------------
od600_planktonic        Optical density at 600nm (planktonic growth)
log10_cfu_ml            Log10 colony forming units per mL (viable cells)
biofilm_od570           Crystal violet biofilm assay (OD570)
metabolic_rfu           Resazurin metabolic activity (relative fluorescence units)

DERIVED OUTCOMES
----------------
pct_inhibition_od600    Percent inhibition of planktonic growth vs vehicle
pct_inhibition_biofilm  Percent inhibition of biofilm vs vehicle
pct_inhibition_metabolic Percent inhibition of metabolic activity vs vehicle

NOTES:
- Three biological replicates each with three technical replicates
- Plate effects may influence results (consider as covariate)
- Standard MIC = lowest concentration with ≥90% growth inhibition
- Multiple comparisons across strains and timepoints
- Consider dose-response modeling (4-parameter logistic)
